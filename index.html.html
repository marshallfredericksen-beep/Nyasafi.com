Our Mission

What Is NYASAFI?

NYASAFI empowers smallholder farmers and fishers in Kyela, Tanzania, through sustainable systems including integrated agriculture-aquaculture and aquaponics, to achieve post-harvest profit improvements and food diversification. By training communities, establishing market linkages, and introducing diverse crop-fish systems, NYASAFI builds climate-resilient livelihoods, reduces food insecurity, and creates sustainable, locally-owned food systems for women, youth, and rural households.

Three Core Goals
1

Integrated Systems (Agriculture & Aquaculture)

We promote aquaculture integrated systems and aquaponics to co-produce fish and diverse crops.

2

Improve Market Access & Reduce Post-Harvest Losses

We improve market connections and reduce losses through cold storage, better packaging, transport, and reliable market linkages that connect farmers and fishers to buyers.

3

Food and Nutrition Diversity

We promote crop and fish diversity to improve household nutrition and strengthen resilient healthy communities.

Innovation & Approach

Our Approach

NYASAFI's model addresses chronic food insecurity, limited market access, and low dietary diversity facing rural communities in Tanzania's southern highlands through four interconnected pillars.

Train

Build skills in aquaculture, aquaponics, crop diversity & business management among smallholder farmers and fishers.

Produce

Co-produce fish and crops using integrated, sustainable agriculture-aquaculture systems at community scale.

Preserve

Reduce post-harvest losses through cold storage, improved packaging & quality handling infrastructure.

Connect

Link farmers and fishers to reliable anchor institutions (schools, hospitals, and government agencies) for consistent income & growth.

What Makes NYASAFI Novel

Community-Scale Aquaponics

Fish-waste-to-plant fertilization systems have rarely been deployed at community scale in Tanzania's Lake Nyasa region. NYASAFI does exactly this.

Post-Harvest + Production

Unlike standalone food-production projects, NYASAFI adds a post-harvest profit dimension typically absent from food systems work.

Localized Cooperative Structures

Training is localized with integrated cooperative frameworks, building community ownership that global models often miss.

Anchor Institution Connections

Linking farmers directly to schools, hospitals, and government agencies creates reliable, stable demand for their produce and fish.

Our Impact

Why It Matters

NYASAFI's integrated approach delivers measurable change across economic, nutritional, and environmental dimensions for rural communities.

Increased Income

Higher post-harvest profits and diverse income sources for farmers and fishers through improved market access and reduced losses.

Improved Nutrition

More diverse, affordable, and available fish and crops on household plates, improving food security for women, children, and rural families.

Reduced Losses

Significant reduction in post-harvest losses through improved handling & storage infrastructure, cold chain investment, and better packaging.

Climate Resilience

Sustainable systems that conserve resources, reduce pressure on wild fish stocks in Lake Nyasa, and help communities adapt to climate variability.

Who We Serve

Beneficiaries & Community Impact

NYASAFI centers women, youth, and rural households as both active participants and direct beneficiaries, not just recipients of aid.

Women farmers & fishers Rural youth Smallholder farmers Smallholder fishers Schools & institutions Rural households

Direct benefits include increased household income, improved dietary diversity, reduced food waste, and access to reliable markets. Environmental benefits include reduced pressure on wild fish stocks in Lake Nyasa.

NYASAFI addresses the reality that rural communities, particularly women and youth, lack the technical skills and economic infrastructure to adapt to climate variability and resource depletion.
